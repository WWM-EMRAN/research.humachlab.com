Pathway Philosophy

Every opportunity should produce visible learning evidence and useful HUMACH outputs.

The goal is not only to assign tasks, but to guide contributors toward portfolio-ready work: a research summary, an experiment, a dashboard, a prototype, a documentation contribution, a publication-support task, or a collaboration concept.

Clear pathway

Research, development, thesis, volunteer, and collaboration pathways are separated so applicants can choose the right entry point.

Milestone-based

Each pathway should move through practical milestones instead of vague participation.

Evidence-first

Outputs should be visible through reports, code, figures, documentation, prototypes, or publication support.

Collaborative

Students, researchers, developers, and partners can contribute at different levels depending on readiness and project fit.

Application Flow

From Expression of Interest to Visible Output

01

Express Interest

Submit the form with your interests, skills, and preferred pathway.

02

Match a Pathway

Your background is matched with a research, development, thesis, volunteer, or collaboration direction.

03

Define Tasks

Milestones are defined so the work is practical, measurable, and suitable for your level.

04

Build Evidence

You produce evidence such as reports, code, dashboards, figures, prototypes, or writing support.
